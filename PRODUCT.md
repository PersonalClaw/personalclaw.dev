# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is the technically fluent owner-operator evaluating whether to run an AI agent platform on their own machine. They already use editors, terminals, model APIs, and self-hosted tools, but they want one dependable place to delegate work, inspect autonomous runs, and retain control of their data and vendors.

A secondary audience is open-source builders and app authors deciding whether PersonalClaw is a credible platform to extend. They need to see an understandable architecture, a real app boundary, and evidence that third-party capabilities are treated as products rather than loose scripts.

## Product Purpose

The site introduces PersonalClaw, demonstrates the shipped product with real captures, explains how its agentic operating system fits together, and gives a technically serious visitor enough confidence to install or inspect the source.

Success means a visitor understands within one pass that PersonalClaw is not another hosted chat wrapper. It is a self-hosted system for chat, autonomous goal loops, memory, knowledge, automation, and removable apps, all behind one gateway the owner controls.

## Positioning

The personal AI agent platform you fully own: one local gateway, observable autonomy, and replaceable vendors.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: **Get PersonalClaw**, linking to the public GitHub repository and installation path.
- Secondary CTA: **Explore the system**, moving visitors into the product tour before they commit.
- The line to remember: **One agentic OS. Your machine. Your rules.**
- Belief ladder:
  1. PersonalClaw does real work across chat, goals, knowledge, tasks, and automation.
  2. Autonomous work remains visible, pausable, and bounded by deterministic guardrails.
  3. Conversations, memory, knowledge, and app state live on infrastructure the owner controls.
  4. Models, search, speech, channels, and agent runtimes are removable apps rather than permanent dependencies.
  5. The system is open to extension through a scanner-gated, permission-aware app platform.
  6. The project is honest about its pre-1.0 lifecycle and current limits.
- Proof on hand:
  - Real, reproducible light and dark product captures in the core repository.
  - An MIT-licensed public codebase with a documented v0.1.0 release.
  - Zero product telemetry.
  - Thirty-six first-party apps spanning models, search, speech, agents, tools, channels, and full contributed interfaces.
  - Published architecture and security documents covering the gateway, app lifecycle, approval controls, scoped tokens, egress, and supply-chain scanning.

## Brand Personality

Warm, mechanical, optimistic. PersonalClaw feels like a capable machine working beside one person in a quiet night studio. It is technically rigorous without performing technicality, playful without becoming toy-like, and confident without hiding tradeoffs.

The voice uses plain, active language. It names concrete capabilities and boundaries instead of relying on abstract AI claims.

## Anti-references

- Hacker-terminal cosplay: green-on-black, scanlines, CRT effects, or unreadable code theater.
- Generic AI SaaS: purple-blue glows, floating gradient shapes, centered copy over an empty mesh, and identical feature cards.
- Editorial tech affectation: serif headlines, mono micro-labels, ruled broadsheets, and numbered section scaffolding.
- Enterprise fleet software: alarm-wall density, cold blue palettes, and language aimed at procurement committees.
- Warm-paper craft branding: beige backgrounds, brass accents, and artisan metaphors unrelated to the product.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the product early.** Real captures carry the argument before feature copy does.
2. **Explain ownership as architecture.** Local-first, provider-agnostic, and app-based claims are shown through concrete system relationships.
3. **Make autonomy legible.** Loops, approvals, state, and guardrails are presented as observable machinery rather than magic.
4. **Use warmth as signal.** Coral marks active intelligence, focus, and primary action; it is not ambient decoration.
5. **Be candid about maturity.** Pre-1.0 status and operational limits are visible without dominating the value proposition.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text must meet at least 4.5:1 contrast, interface graphics and focus indicators at least 3:1, and every interactive element must be keyboard reachable with a visible focus state. Touch targets are at least 44 by 44 CSS pixels. Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`; the complete narrative and every control remain usable without animation. Content reflows cleanly at 200% zoom and down to a 320px viewport.
